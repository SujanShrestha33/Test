Art

The term "arts" refers to incorporating several artistic expressions within the early childhood education curriculum, including visual arts, music, dance, theatre, and literature.

Drama And Puppetry

Puppetry and drama both entail the use of puppets for role-playing and storytelling, but drama incorporates the use of body language, gestures, facial emotions, and voice.

Moverment and Music

Children's physical, cognitive, social, and emotional development can benefit greatly from the inclusion of music and movement in early childhood education.

Technology

The inclusion of technology in the curriculum can foster young children's creativity by providing them with possibilities for learning, discovery, and expression.

Engineering

Engineering is the application of mathematical and scientific techniques to plan, create, and manage systems, devices, and processes that address issues or enhance current ones

Science

Early childhood education's science curriculum strongly emphasises encouraging young children to develop their scientific thinking and enquiry abilities (Mayesky, 2015).

Language and Literacy

language and literacy development in early infancy is important for a child's growth and development.

Mathematics and numeracy

Young children's creativity is greatly influenced by math and numeracy. Children's creativity may be improved by a variety of arithmetic and numeracy-related activities, such as investigating patterns, forms, and sizes

Humanities and Social Sciences

Young children's creativity can be enhanced through the humanities and social science curriculum through the study of subjects like history, geography, social studies, and cultural studies.

Integrated Curriculum

An integrated curriculum is an educational approach that combines several topics, including Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics (STEM), the Arts (STEAM), and Reading (STREAM).
